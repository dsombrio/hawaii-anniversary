Why September / October?

Late September through October is Hawaii's shoulder season — summer crowds have thinned, prices drop 10–20% from peak, and the weather is still dry and warm (80s, low humidity). Ocean conditions are calmer than winter months. October means fewer crowds at Pearl Harbor, Hanauma Bay, and the best restaurants. Trade-off: tail end of hurricane season (officially ends November 1) — but direct-hit odds are very low. Book the Royal Hawaiian or Halekulani 3–4 months out for best rates.

Our Recommendation

For an Anniversary: Option A — The Royal Hawaiian

An anniversary is a time to be spoiled, not to think. Option A's all-inclusive feel means you're never pulling out a credit card at lunch — you just show up, the resort handles it, and you focus on each other. The Mai Tai Bar at the Royal Hawaiian alone is worth it — it's the most romantic bar in the Pacific. Option B is better for food-lovers and repeat visitors who want to explore. For a special trip? The Pink Palace earns its legend.
